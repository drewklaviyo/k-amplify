Pro tip

Write updates rich with leading/input metrics — not just "we shipped X." In the example above, "accounts researched/day" and "meetings booked from agent leads" are input metrics that predict whether PPR will move. If your weekly update only says what you built but not what it did, it's not enough. Define your 2-3 leading metrics per project and track them weekly.

Monthly Execution Review (MER)
Last week of month 60 min
Amplify leadership + all PMs + Eng leads

The "state of Amplify" meeting. Deep review of progress against quarterly goals, organized by partner org. Goal updates must be written before this meeting — the MER is a discussion, not a presentation. Drew reads your updates in advance; the meeting is for decisions, escalations, and cross-cutting tradeoffs.

After MER Async
PMs → partner stakeholders

One-page summary per partner org, posted to #amplify-x-[org] Slack channels. Generated from your goal update — not a separate artifact. What shipped, what's next, metrics, asks/blockers.

Pro tip

Don't wait for MER to update your partners. Run bi-weekly syncs with your partner's core group (e.g., Sales Ops lead, CS Ops lead) so there are no surprises at MER. The monthly digest confirms what partners already know — it doesn't break news. If your partner is surprised by your MER update, you're behind.

Discovery Hours
Monthly Ongoing
Every PM

Spend time in your partner's world every month. Shadow sales calls, listen to Gong recordings, sit in on their team standups, pull their data, watch how they use (or don't use) what you've built. The goal is to see their reality firsthand — not filtered through a status update or a Slack thread.

Capture one insight that changes how you think about your roadmap. Bring it to PM Sync.

Artifact: Insight shared at PM Sync
Jay: Shadowed 3 BDR calls this week. Reps are copy-pasting enrichment data from the agent into SFDC manually because the auto-sync only updates overnight. If we push real-time sync, that's ~10 min saved per account — across 100 BDRs that's 16 hrs/day. Moving this up in the backlog. Tyler: Sat in on CS team standup. CSMs are building their own account health spreadsheets because they don't trust the agent's scoring. Need to dig into why — scheduling 3 ride-alongs next week to watch them work.
Why discovery matters

The best product decisions come from direct observation, not secondhand reports. If you haven't been in your partner's world this month, your roadmap is based on assumptions. Discovery hours are how you pressure-test those assumptions.

Quarterly Planning
Last 3 weeks of quarter Multiple sessions
All PMs + Eng leads + Drew

Set next quarter's commitments per partner org. The process:

1. Gather partner input (weeks 1-2): Each PM gathers needs from meetings and core group syncs with their partner stakeholders. What do they need most? What's changed? What's underperforming? Collect this into a prioritized list.

2. Draft the plan (week 3): Each PM writes a one-page quarterly plan for their goal: what they'll commit to, the milestones, the metrics they'll move, and what they need (eng capacity, system access, partner commitment).

3. Planning review (week 4): All PMs + Drew review plans together. Resolve cross-cutting tradeoffs (capacity, shared infra). Finalize commitments. Update Linear: set milestones, update goal descriptions with new targets.

The Rules

Non-negotiable standards for every Amplify PM.

Rule 1
Every project ties to a goal

No orphan projects. If your project doesn't connect to one of the goals under "Working AI First," it either needs to be connected or it shouldn't exist.

Rule 2
Linear is the source. Everything else is a view.

No slides or presentations. Roadmaps and updates live in Linear Docs, Google Docs, or webpages generated from Linear. When things change, you update Linear — not a separate artifact.

Rule 3
Projects need milestones

At least one milestone per quarter per project. Name them clearly (e.g., "BDR Intelligence POC — Go/No-Go" not "M1").

Rule 4
Friday project updates are non-negotiable

Every Friday. Every active project. 4:00 PM calendar block. 10 minutes. Metric impact with leading indicators, blockers, next week. Set the health indicator.

Rule 5
Goal updates come before MER, not after

Write your goal-level status update before the Monthly Execution Review. The MER discusses your update — it's not the place to present it for the first time.

Rule 6
Issues go in the right team

Work for Sales goes in Amplify Sales. Work for Support goes in Amplify Support. If an issue spans teams, put it in the primary team and link related issues.

Rule 7
Use cycles consistently

Every active issue should be in the current cycle. Don't let issues float in backlog while engineers are actually working on them.

Rule 8
At Risk / Off Track triggers a conversation, not a punishment

If you set a project or goal to At Risk or Off Track, bring it to PM Sync on Monday. Drew will help unblock, escalate, or re-scope. The worst thing you can do is mark something On Track when it isn't — that's how partners lose trust.

FAQ

Common questions, especially if you're new to Amplify.

▶ Where exactly do I post standups?
In your team's Slack channel: #amplify-sales, #amplify-support, #amplify-cs, #amplify-rnd, or #amplify-marketing. Post by 10 AM local. Three lines, no thread — just drop it in the channel.
▶ How do I write a status update in Linear?
Open your project in Linear → click the "Updates" tab (or the status icon next to the project name) → click "New update." Set the health indicator (On Track / At Risk / Off Track), then write using the three-part format: metric impact, blockers, next week. Same flow for goal-level updates on your sub-initiative.
▶ How do I know which projects are mine?
Your projects live in the Linear team for your partner org (e.g., "Amplify R&D" for Christina). Look at the projects under your team — those are yours. If you're new, your first task is to audit what exists and confirm with Drew. If projects are missing, create them. If there are orphans, connect them to your goal or archive them.
▶ What are "leading metrics" and how do I find them?
Your goal has a lagging metric (e.g., "PPR +30%") that moves slowly. Leading metrics are the inputs you can track weekly that predict whether the lagging metric will move. Examples: for Sales, it's accounts researched/day, meetings booked, BDR active rate. For Support, it's AI resolution rate, false positive rate, ticket categories covered. For CS, it's CSM time-in-tool, accounts auto-triaged, health score accuracy. Define 2-3 leading metrics per project in your first week and track them in every Friday update. If you don't know yours yet, talk to Drew.
▶ What happens when I set something to At Risk or Off Track?
Bring it to PM Sync on Monday. The team will help: escalate a blocker, re-scope, shift capacity, or adjust the timeline. Setting At Risk early is a sign of good judgment, not failure. Setting On Track when you know it's not — that's the problem. See Rule 8.
▶ How do partner requests come in? Is there an intake process?
Partner requests come from meetings and core group syncs — not a ticket queue. You're embedded with your partner org, so you hear needs in real time. Capture them, triage at PM Sync on Monday, and every request gets a response within one sprint: "yes and here's when," "not now, here's why," or "let's discuss." Don't let requests sit unacknowledged.
▶ What should I prepare for PM Sync on Monday?
Three things: (1) one thing that changed for your partner this week, (2) any capacity asks or tradeoffs you need help with, and (3) any intake items to triage. You don't need a doc — just come ready to talk. If something is At Risk or Off Track, lead with that.
▶ Who is my eng lead and what's their role?
Each Linear team has an eng lead paired with the PM. The eng lead owns the technical execution: sprint capacity, issue breakdown, code review, technical decisions. You own the what and why (scope, priorities, partner alignment); they own the how (technical approach, estimates, team assignments). You write the Friday project update; they can contribute but you're accountable. You run sprint planning together.
▶ Should I be meeting with my partner org outside of MER?
Yes — and this is what separates good from great. Every PM should have a regular cadence with their partner's core group (e.g., Sales Ops lead, CS Ops lead, Support Eng lead). At minimum bi-weekly, ideally weekly for active partners like Sales and CS. These aren't in the "required" cadence because the format varies per org — but if your partner is ever surprised by your MER update, you're not meeting enough. Set these up in your first two weeks.
▶ What does a Monthly Partner Digest look like?
It's a Slack post — not a doc, not a deck. 10-15 lines max. Structure: status (On Track / At Risk / Off Track), what shipped, key metrics, what's next, what you need from them. See the example in the Monthly section above. Keep it scannable — your partner VP should be able to read it in 30 seconds.
▶ What formats do we use instead of slides?
Linear Docs, Google Docs, or webpages generated from Linear. No slide decks. If you're presenting at a QBR or leadership review, your data comes from your goal updates and project statuses in Linear. The doc is the presentation.
▶ I'm new — what do I do in my first two weeks?
Week 1: Audit your Linear team — what projects exist, what's their status, are they connected to your goal? Read the last month of project updates. Meet your eng lead. Meet Drew 1:1 to align on your goal's target metrics and leading indicators. Week 2: Set up your partner meetings (bi-weekly minimum with core group). Write your first Friday project updates. Define your 2-3 leading metrics per project. Post your first standup. You should feel oriented by end of week 2.
