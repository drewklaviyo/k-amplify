Amplify Operating Rhythm

How every Amplify PM, engineer, and designer operates — daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly. Centered on Linear, with continuous planning built in.

Our Mission

Amplify exists to drive outsize business outcomes by building AI and technology that fits Klaviyo like a glove — designed around how our people actually work, succeed, and create value. Role performance. Operational efficiency. Business results.

Why We Build

We build because Klaviyo doesn’t succeed the way other companies succeed. Off-the-shelf tools optimize for the average company. We optimize for this company.

Buy gets you 80% of the way, fast. Build gets you 100% of the way, forever. We choose build in areas where Klaviyo’s way of working is the competitive advantage — because a third-party product can’t encode what makes us different.

When we build, we own the interface between our people and their work. We can adapt it, evolve it, and compound our advantage over time. We accept slower starts for compounding returns: tools that fit our data, our processes, and our people exactly.

How We Plan: Continuous Pipeline

Every team maintains a prioritized board. Whoever has capacity pulls from the top. Teams are collectively responsible for quarterly commitments — but priorities can shift freely within the quarter.

Project Pipeline Stages

Every project flows through these stages in Linear. The goal: solid upfront understanding of the problem, rapid prototyping and testing of hypotheses, and launching iteratively.

StageWho DrivesWhat Happens
IdentifyPM + DesignUnderstand the problem. PM and Design do discovery — partner interviews, data analysis, user research.
DefinePM + Design + EngScope the solution. Design creates prototypes, PM writes requirements. Eng assesses feasibility and dependencies.
BuildEng + DesignEngineers build. Design reviews and iterates. Whoever has capacity pulls from the top of the queue.
ShipEng + PMLaunch iteratively. Measure impact. Validate with partners.
LearnTriadRetrospect and improve. What worked, what didn't, what changes for the next cycle.
Design is embedded

Designers are embedded into initiatives and projects (added as members). When design is needed, issues are created with type "Design" and attached to respective milestones. Designers participate in all team and project rituals.

On-Demand Swarming

A time-boxed, PM-led working session where a cross-functional team drives rapid progress on a critical project. Not a status meeting — a decision-making and unblocking session where work actually happens.

When to activate

A project hits a critical phase (launch prep, major milestone approaching), is at risk or falling behind, or needs acceleration. PM decides to activate, or Drew flags it. Swarms are temporary — deactivate when the milestone ships or the project stabilizes. If it runs for more than 4–6 weeks, the project scope is too big.

Swarm Session
2–3x / week 30 min
PM (leads) + Eng Lead + Engineers + Ops + Designer + SMEs — whoever is on the critical path

The team

  • Core: PM (owner), eng lead, 1–2 engineers, ops lead, designer (if relevant)
  • Extended: SMEs, partner stakeholders, data science — anyone on the critical path

The plan

Every swarm has a shared goal + plan doc (Linear doc, Notion, Google doc — doesn’t matter). Linked from the project, updated every session. Single source of truth for what the swarm is driving toward.

Session format (30 min)

PhaseTimeWhat happens
Goal check2 minReview the plan. Where are we? What's left?
Blockers + decisions10 minSurface what's stuck. Make decisions live. No "let's take this offline."
Work together15 minCut issues, assign work, cut scope, pair on problems. Real-time collaboration.
Update the plan3 minMark what's done, what changed, what's next. Post brief note to Linear.

Rules

  • The PM owns the swarm. They activate it, run it, and kill it when it’s done.
  • Decisions are made in the room. If a decision-maker isn’t in the swarm, invite them or escalate.
  • The plan is updated every session — no stale plans.
  • Swarms are temporary. If it’s been running 4–6 weeks, re-evaluate scope.

Monthly Execution Review (MER)
Last week of month 60 min
Amplify leadership + all PMs + Eng leads

The "state of Amplify" meeting. Deep review of progress against quarterly goals, organized by partner org. Goal updates must be written before this meeting — the MER is a discussion, not a presentation. Drew reads your updates in advance; the meeting is for decisions, escalations, and cross-cutting tradeoffs.

Pro tip

Don't wait for MER to update your partners. Run bi-weekly syncs with your partner's core group (e.g., Sales Ops lead, CS Ops lead) so there are no surprises at MER. The monthly goal update confirms what partners already know — it doesn't break news. If your partner is surprised by your MER update, you're behind.

The Rules

Non-negotiable standards for every Amplify PM.

Rule 1
Every project ties to a goal

No orphan projects. If your project doesn't connect to one of the goals under "Working AI First," it either needs to be connected or it shouldn't exist.

Rule 2
Linear is the source. Everything else is a view.

No slides or presentations. Roadmaps and updates live in Linear Docs, Google Docs, or webpages generated from Linear. When things change, you update Linear — not a separate artifact.

Rule 3
Projects need milestones

At least one milestone per quarter per project. Name them clearly (e.g., "BDR Intelligence POC — Go/No-Go" not "M1").

Rule 4
Friday project updates are non-negotiable

Every Friday. Every active project. 4:00 PM calendar block. 10 minutes. Metric impact with leading indicators, blockers, next week. Set the health indicator.

Rule 5
Goal updates come before MER, not after

Write your goal-level status update before the Monthly Execution Review. The MER discusses your update — it's not the place to present it for the first time.

Rule 6
Issues go in the right team

Work for Sales goes in Amplify Sales. Work for Support goes in Amplify Support. If an issue spans teams, put it in the primary team and link related issues.

Rule 7
Keep the board honest

If someone is working on it, it should be In Progress. If it's blocked, mark it Blocked. Don't let the board lie — it's how the triad spots problems.

Rule 8
At Risk / Off Track triggers a conversation, not a punishment

If you set a project or goal to At Risk or Off Track, bring it to PM Sync on Monday. Drew will help unblock, escalate, or re-scope. The worst thing you can do is mark something On Track when it isn't — that's how partners lose trust.

FAQ

Common questions, especially if you're new to Amplify.

▶ Where exactly do I post standups?
Mon, Tue, Thu: In-person with your team. Wed, Fri: Async in your team's Slack channel: #amplify-sales, #amplify-support, #amplify-cs, #amplify-rnd, or #amplify-marketing. Post by 10 AM local. Three lines, no thread — just drop it in the channel.
▶ How do I write a status update in Linear?
Open your project in Linear → click the "Updates" tab (or the status icon next to the project name) → click "New update." Set the health indicator (On Track / At Risk / Off Track), then write using the three-part format: metric impact, blockers, next week. Same flow for goal-level updates on your sub-initiative.
▶ How do I know which projects are mine?
Your projects live in the Linear team for your partner org (e.g., "Amplify R&D" for Christina). Look at the projects under your team — those are yours. If you're new, your first task is to audit what exists and confirm with Drew. If projects are missing, create them. If there are orphans, connect them to your goal or archive them.
▶ What are "leading metrics" and how do I find them?
Your goal has a lagging metric (e.g., "PPR +30%") that moves slowly. Leading metrics are the inputs you can track weekly that predict whether the lagging metric will move. Examples: for Sales, it's accounts researched/day, meetings booked, BDR active rate. For Support, it's AI resolution rate, false positive rate, ticket categories covered. For CS, it's CSM time-in-tool, accounts auto-triaged, health score accuracy. Define 2-3 leading metrics per project in your first week and track them in every Friday update. If you don't know yours yet, talk to Drew.
▶ What happens when I set something to At Risk or Off Track?
Bring it to PM Sync on Monday. The team will help: escalate a blocker, re-scope, shift capacity, or adjust the timeline. Setting At Risk early is a sign of good judgment, not failure. Setting On Track when you know it's not — that's the problem. See Rule 8.
▶ How do partner requests come in? Is there an intake process?
Partner requests come from meetings and core group syncs — not a ticket queue. You're embedded with your partner org, so you hear needs in real time. Capture them, add to your Backlog, triage at PM Sync on Monday. Every request gets a response within two weeks: "yes and here's when," "not now, here's why," or "let's discuss." Don't let requests sit unacknowledged.
▶ What should I prepare for PM Sync on Monday?
Three things: (1) one thing that changed for your partner this week, (2) any capacity asks or tradeoffs you need help with, and (3) any intake items to triage. You don't need a doc — just come ready to talk. If something is At Risk or Off Track, lead with that.
▶ Who is my eng lead and what's their role?
Each team is led by a triad: PM + Eng Lead + Design Lead (4 of 6 teams currently have all three). The PM owns the what and why (scope, priorities, partner alignment). The eng lead owns the how (technical approach, issue breakdown, code review). The design lead shapes solutions in the Identify and Define stages. The triad reviews the board together regularly. You write the Friday project update; the triad can contribute but the PM is accountable.
▶ Should I be meeting with my partner org outside of MER?
Yes — and this is what separates good from great. Every PM should have a regular cadence with their partner's core group (e.g., Sales Ops lead, CS Ops lead, Support Eng lead). At minimum bi-weekly, ideally weekly for active partners like Sales and CS. These aren't in the "required" cadence because the format varies per org — but if your partner is ever surprised by your MER update, you're not meeting enough. Set these up in your first two weeks.
▶ What formats do we use instead of slides?
Linear Docs, Google Docs, or webpages generated from Linear. No slide decks. If you're presenting at a QBR or leadership review, your data comes from your goal updates and project statuses in Linear. The doc is the presentation.
▶ I'm new — what do I do in my first two weeks?
Week 1: Audit your Linear team — what projects exist, what's their status, are they connected to your goal? Read the last month of project updates. Meet your eng lead. Meet Drew 1:1 to align on your goal's target metrics and leading indicators. Week 2: Set up your partner meetings (bi-weekly minimum with core group). Write your first Friday project updates. Define your 2-3 leading metrics per project. Post your first standup. You should feel oriented by end of week 2.
